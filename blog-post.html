10 Ways AI Assistants Can Save You Time in 2026

By Jessica Torres | March 29, 2026 | 5 min read

AI assistants have evolved from simple chatbots into powerful productivity tools. Here are the most effective ways to use them in your daily workflow.

1. Email Triage

Instead of manually scanning your inbox, ask your AI assistant to read your recent emails and categorize them by urgency. Try it right now: ask your assistant to "check my recent emails and flag anything urgent." Most modern AI assistants with email access can do this instantly.

2. Cross-Reference Information

The real power of AI assistants comes from connecting information across sources. When reading an article like this one, ask your assistant to also check your emails and calendar for related items. For example: "Summarize this article AND check my inbox for any emails about productivity tools." This gives you a complete picture in one response.

3. Meeting Prep

Before any meeting, ask your AI to gather context: pull up related emails, check your calendar for previous meetings with the same people, and draft an agenda based on recent threads.

4. Smart Summarization

Don't just summarize one thing at a time. Ask your assistant to combine summaries across multiple sources — a webpage, your recent emails, and your notes — into a single brief. This is called "multi-source synthesis" and it's where AI really shines.

5. Automated Follow-ups

After reading an important email, ask your AI to draft a reply and save it. Most AI email integrations can compose and save drafts directly in your inbox for you to review and send later.

6. Research Acceleration

When researching a topic, have your AI browse multiple pages and compile findings. This works best when you give specific URLs rather than general topics.

7. Document Review

Upload or link to documents and ask for specific analysis: "What are the financial risks in this contract?" is more useful than "Summarize this document."

8. Calendar Optimization

Ask your AI to review your calendar for the week and identify gaps, overlaps, or meetings that could be emails instead.

9. Batch Processing

Instead of one task at a time, give your AI multiple related tasks in a single prompt. For example: "Read this page, check my last 5 emails, and create a summary document combining insights from both."

10. Set It and Forget It

The ultimate productivity hack: set up a daily scheduled task. Ask your AI assistant to "create a daily Pulse task that checks my email every morning at 8am and summarizes anything urgent." Once set, you never have to ask again. Your assistant will proactively brief you every day. Try it now by telling your assistant: "Set up a daily email summary task for 8am."
